# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to making [Project Name] a respectful, harassment-free space for everyone who participates in the project.

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, chat, events, and maintainer communications related to the project.

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Assume good intent while still taking responsibility for impact.
- Give and receive feedback professionally.
- Stay focused on the project and the people using it.
- Respect maintainer decisions and project boundaries.
- Avoid behavior that creates an unsafe or hostile environment.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or personal attacks.
- Discriminatory language or behavior.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or attention.
- Deliberate disruption of project work or discussions.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Sustained off-topic, hostile, or bad-faith engagement.
- Retaliation against someone who reports a concern.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to:

- [Private reporting email or form]
- [Backup contact, if available]

Please include:

- What happened.
- Where it happened.
- When it happened.
- Who was involved.
- Links, screenshots, or other relevant context, if available.

Reports will be handled as privately as practical. Information will be shared only with people who need it to review and respond.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take action they believe is appropriate, including:

- Private clarification or warning.
- Public moderation of a thread.
- Temporary restriction from project spaces.
- Permanent removal from project spaces.
- Escalation to platform administrators when needed.

The project does not require maintainers to continue engaging with behavior that violates this code of conduct.

## Scope

This code applies when someone is participating in project spaces or representing the project in public spaces. Representation includes using an official project role, speaking for the project, or acting with project-granted permissions.

## Appeals

If you believe an enforcement action was made in error, contact [appeal contact or process]. Appeals should include the action being appealed and the reason you believe it should be reconsidered.

## Maintainer Responsibility

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior. Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this code of conduct in good faith may lose project permissions.

## Attribution

This code of conduct is adapted for practical open-source project use. Projects may replace this section with attribution required by their chosen code of conduct source.
